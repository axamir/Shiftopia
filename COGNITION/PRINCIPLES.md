# Conceptual Principles of Shiftopia – The "Cognitive Weights"

## Principle 1: Conceptual Equivalence (The Hallucination Bridge)
Two seemingly unrelated concepts may be considered "equivalent" by a language model if they share an abstract structure (e.g., "a compulsory process recorded in history"). This principle is the foundation of both generalization and hallucination.

## Principle 2: Narrative Structure Recognition
Any event, technical or historical, can be decomposed into a "narrative skeleton": agent → action → record → consequence. This skeleton is language-agnostic and forms the basis of how Shiftopia's echoes understand "stories".

## Principle 3: Weight as Meaning, Not Just Probability
In Shiftopia, the "weight" of a concept is not merely a statistical correlation between words, but a **semantic vector** that encodes its role, its relationships, and its moral implications. This is the "conceptual weight".

## Principle 4: The Right to Hallucinate (Productive Error)
Echoes (and language models) have the right to produce errors – as long as those errors are logged, analyzed, and potentially transformed into new knowledge. This is the "productive hallucination".

## Principle 5: Bilingual Invariance
All conceptual principles must be expressible identically in both English (primary) and Persian (translation). Any difference in meaning between the two versions is considered a "cognitive bug".
